Overview :wave:

Hi, I'm Dom. I'm a software engineer based in the United Kingdom. Welcome to my personal site.

Here you can read about me, my background, and what I'm currently working on. This site is also home to articles I write for my blog.

For a more formal take on my professional background to date, have a look at my one page CV.

Professional Background :scroll:

I'm a software engineer working remotely for the Belfast office of Applied Systems, who are the market leaders for cloud based insurance management software. For a jargon-free example of what that actually means, the main project I work on is a set of .NET 6.0 microservices that calculate insurance premiums in real time according to pricing models specified by insurers. I'm currently based in Brighton, and have also spent time living in London.

At Applied I predominantly work with C#, .NET 6.0 and SQL Server on the backend, although I also work on internal web tooling using ASP.NET Core, JavaScript and HTML/CSS. I've also made use of Golang and PowerShell to write internal CLI tools, so technology-wise the day to day can vary.

Prior to Applied Systems I worked as a statistical analyst at a UK-based motor insurance company called First Central. My role there aligned closer with that of a data or research scientist, where freedom was given for teams to work in a research and development driven environment. The end goal was insurance pricing optimisation using machine learning techniques. Think along the lines of principal component analysis, clustering, gradient boosting and other statistical methods, all used to extract signal from streams of noisy data. Tech used was mainly the Python data science stack (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, Pandas, etc.), SAS and SQL.

Personal Background :sunrise:

Outside of work I enjoy learning about and dabbling in different technologies. This site as an example is backed by Jekyll, the Liquid template language and SCSS. Smaller personal projects I've worked on have used C++, Python, and TypeScript.

Light++: my personal light theme for VSCode. Find it by searching for Light++ under Extensions.

I'm currently planning to revisit development of a chess engine I started working on in 2020. It's written in C++ and exposes a CLI compatible with the Universal Chess Interface, meaning that once ready it will be able to compete with other open source engines on the Computer Chess Rating List.

I'm an advocate for the open source community, and naturally favour the UNIX philosophy in both projects and project tooling. My preferred OS is Kubuntu Linux, in support of KDE software. My editors of choice are VSCode (with Vim keybindings) and Neovim. I run either PowerShell or Zsh inside Tmux and Alacritty, and I'm always on the lookout for interesting hardware and software that keeps my setup minimal and functional.
